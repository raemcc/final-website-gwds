Reflecting on this project

I have included a few reflection paragraphs on each page of this website, but I will centralise them here. . I completed the entirety of the CARP task in one day, and the typography tasks in two. This was more due to the fact I was left with very limited time for this whole project because I have been so ill. I would have liked to have more time to expand upon my evaluation of the individual pieces and try out some different layout ideas for the typography pieces.

I initially wasn't drawn to the typography task; in general I prefer the more structured coding work. I found I enjoyed the challenge of the limited colour palette as it let me focus more on exactly how captivating my typography was. I would say some were more successful than others, I think I am particularly drawn to the ones with the more interesting backgrounds, i.e. the black and green. I think the random system was my favourite style to complete, and I think this abstract style of art is very interesting to me and something I will hopefully research a bit more.

I really enjoyed completing the coding worksheets, I found them really helpful to look back on while I was making this very website! I didn't follow every instruction exactly - if I felt I had the general gist of something I didn't think there was much point repeating myself. I also did not complete the tasks centred around google fonts because that seemed pretty straightforward, and I wasnâ€™t too interested in implementing them in my site.

I enjoyed learning about the Design Principles; at times I did find it hard to know when to stop, as it feels like I could always find some minute detail to improve upon. I found the learning curve to working with InDesign a little steeper than some of the other software programs but by the end I was quite enjoying it. I think when I came to doing the second drafts/iterations, an increase in the quality of my work can be seen for most of the posters.

Making this website has definitely developed my coding skills the most, far more than completing the worksheets or watching tutorials. I feel like I now have a pretty good understanding of flexbox and how to make something that kind of works. Its not perfect but I am proud of it for a first project. HTML and CSS are really fun languages to use, and I really enjoy being able to watch my website come to life and begin to navigate it in real time.
