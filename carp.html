Principles of Design

Contrast, Proximity, Repetition & Alignment:

Four of the core principles for good quality designs and layouts. I found the learning curve to working with InDesign a little steeper than some of the other software programs but by the end I was quite enjoying it. I think when I came to doing the second drafts/iterations, an increase in the quality of my work can be seen for most of the posters. For this task I mostly just tried stuff until it seemed to click in my brain, but I found this video by Gareth David Studio useful in diving deeper into these.

Click on the subheadings for a closer look at my final iterations !!

Contrast
I wanted to include multiple different contrasting elements in this poster, specifically I focused pn colours, shapes/textures and fonts. I decided to add a yellow background to make the other colours stand out more and complete the bold primary colour palette. I added black boxes behind the red and blue ones to act as a drop box shadow and also repeated this with the "film" text. I like the 3D effect this provides. I also used big chunky font combined with thinner/lighter text to add further contrast.

Alignment
I used vertical lines in the image of the film reel and lines down the centre to divide the page into sections. I liked the vertical flow and the effect of the central line connecting to the "film" text.

Repetition
I chose to repeat the film festival's name "True/False", I think this is very impactful especially after being highlighted by the red overlay. I also repeated red cicles, and a blue and red colour theme. I think the design is quite cohesive and visually appealing.

Proximity
I think that althought this poster seems simpler than some of the others, it also grabs the viewers attention and draws it right towards the key information. The space around the outside of the design removes visual distractions and allows viewer to focus.
